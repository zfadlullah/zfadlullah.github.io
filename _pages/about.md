Dr. Zubair Md Fadlullah is an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Western University, Canada (since January 2023). He directs the **ACCESS (Advanced Computing and Communication Enabled Smart Society) Research Lab**, focusing on next-generation wireless networks, artificial intelligence, and interdisciplinary research spanning communication systems and smart health technology.

Dr. Fadlullah's research addresses critical challenges at the intersection of communications, computing, and artificial intelligence. His work encompasses 5G and beyond (B5G/6G) networks, Internet of Things (IoT)-based smart systems, federated learning, edge computing, and privacy-preserving machine learning. He has published over 219 peer-reviewed papers in top-tier venues including IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials, IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications, and IEEE IoT Journal.

Prior to joining Western University, Dr. Fadlullah served as Associate Professor at Lakehead University (2019-2022) where he held the prestigious position of Lakehead University-Thunder Bay Regional Health Research Institute Joint Research Chair in Smart Health Technology. He was also Associate Professor (2017-2019) and Assistant Professor (2011-2017) at the Graduate School of Information Sciences, Tohoku University, Japan.

### Impact on Research

**COVID-19 Impact on Research Activities (2020-2022)**

- **March 2020 - August 2020**: Complete loss of lab access due to lockdowns. Estimated 80% reduction in research activity (4 months lost research time).

- **September 2020 - July 2021**: Partial campus access resumed. Students had restricted lab access, international collaborations slowed significantly. Estimated 40% reduction (4 months lost research time).

*Total research time lost to pandemic: Approximately 9.75 months*

## Knowledge & Technology Translation

### Community Engagement (2022-2023)

**Research Chair Initiative - Federated Learning for Remote Communities**

Developed a federated learning framework through dialogue with remote communities, integrated with edge computing and terrestrial-aerial base stations. The system enables:
- Secure, privacy-preserving health data offloading from remote and rural communities
- Localized model training
- Reduced dependency on continuous internet connectivity
- Community empowerment through participatory design and digital inclusion

Target Stakeholder: General Public  
Outcome: Framework enabling equitable access to AI-driven health monitoring for underserved populations

### Technology Development (2019-2022)

**IoT-Based Diagnostic Analytics Platform**

Developed and validated an IoT-based diagnostic analytics platform in collaboration with Thunder Bay Regional Health Research Institute.

Target Stakeholder: Healthcare Personnel  
Outcome: Prototype platform used at TBRHRI for health monitoring and diagnostics
